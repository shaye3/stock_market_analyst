## Executive Summary

---

## Company Overview

Microsoft Corporation is the world's second-largest company by market capitalization (~$2.99 trillion). Its business model spans five major segments: Intelligent Cloud (Azure, SQL Server, Windows Server — ~38% of revenue), Productivity and Business Processes (Microsoft 365, LinkedIn, Dynamics — ~32% of revenue), Personal Computing (Windows, Surface, Xbox — ~25% of revenue), Gaming (Xbox Game Studios), and Enterprise Security (Defender, Sentinel).

The company has successfully transformed from a traditional software vendor into a cloud-first, AI-first platform company. The $69B Activision acquisition in 2023 significantly expanded its gaming division. Most critically, Microsoft's deep OpenAI partnership (40% stake, $13B invested) and Copilot integration across its entire product stack positions it as the primary enterprise AI monetization vehicle. Azure's AI services segment is growing at 60%+ year-over-year, driving total Azure growth of ~33% annually.

Microsoft's competitive moat is among the deepest in technology: switching costs are immense (enterprise customers have Microsoft-embedded workflows across email, office, identity, security, and cloud), brand recognition is unparalleled, and network effects strengthen with each new AI feature integrated across its 400M+ Microsoft 365 subscriber base.

---

## Industry Analysis

The enterprise software and cloud infrastructure market is growing at ~18% CAGR through 2028. AI-driven software spending is the primary growth catalyst. Enterprise AI budgets are projected to triple by 2027 as companies move from experimentation to production deployment. Microsoft is uniquely positioned to capture this spend through its Copilot suite, which is embedded into Word, Excel, Teams, Outlook, GitHub, Dynamics, and Azure — making it the most comprehensive enterprise AI platform.

The cybersecurity market (another Microsoft strength) is growing at 14% CAGR. Cloud infrastructure spending by Fortune 500 companies is projected to reach $1.5 trillion by 2028. Microsoft competes against Google Cloud and Amazon AWS in cloud infrastructure, but its enterprise software moat and existing customer relationships give Azure a structural advantage in enterprise wallet share capture. Competition is intensifying but Microsoft's multi-product platform approach and AI leadership are key differentiators.

## Company Overview

Broadcom Inc. is a $1.6 trillion semiconductor and infrastructure software company. Post-VMware acquisition (closed 2023), Broadcom's revenue is split ~60% semiconductor / 40% infrastructure software. The semiconductor segment designs networking chips, storage controllers, broadband chips, and custom AI accelerators (ASICs). The software segment (VMware + CA Technologies + Symantec enterprise) sells to Fortune 500 companies with extraordinary switching costs.

Broadcom's AI chip business has become the crown jewel of the semiconductor industry. Its custom ASIC designs for Google (TPU v5/v6), Meta (MTIA), and reportedly Apple (for AI inference) represent the highest-margin semiconductor products in existence. CEO Hock Tan has guided to a $60-90B AI-addressable TAM by FY27. This contrasts favorably with NVIDIA's GPU approach — AVGO custom chips are purpose-built for specific workloads, offering 2-5x better energy efficiency than GPUs for inference workloads.

---

## Industry Analysis

The custom AI silicon market is projected to grow from ~$15B in 2024 to $100B+ by 2030. Hyperscalers are increasingly designing their own chips to reduce NVIDIA dependency, creating a massive opportunity for ASIC designers like Broadcom. In parallel, enterprise virtualization (VMware's core) remains mission-critical infrastructure for 80%+ of Fortune 500 companies — AVGO is accelerating VMware's transition to a subscription model, which will dramatically improve revenue quality and margins.
