## 1. Company Overview

Wix.com Ltd. (NASDAQ: WIX) operates a cloud-based web development and business management platform serving small and medium businesses, creators, and professional agencies globally. Founded in Israel in 2006, the company has grown from a consumer-grade website builder to a ~$2B revenue SaaS platform with 68% gross margins.

**Core products and revenue streams:**
- **Wix Editor / Wix Studio**: Flagship drag-and-drop builder (consumer/SMB) and professional agency development platform
- **Wix Harmony**: AI-native website generation platform (launched January 2026) combining natural language creation with manual editing — Wix's direct response to AI disruption
- **Base44**: AI-powered "vibe coding" app builder acquired June 2025 for ~$80M, now at $100M ARR — one of the fastest-growing SaaS products ever recorded
- **Velo by Wix**: Full-stack, no/low-code development environment
- **Business Solutions**: Wix Payments, Wix Bookings, Wix Stores, email marketing — the integrated business operating system layer that drives ARPU expansion
- **Wix App Market / Marketplace**: Third-party ecosystem and freelancer matching

Revenue model is primarily subscription-based with a freemium entry tier. Full-year 2025 revenue reached $1.993B (+13.9% YoY), with Q4 2025 bookings growth of +15% signaling durable demand. CEO Avishai Abrahami has maintained leadership through the company's turbulent 2025 transition year.

The company's strategic pivot is from "website builder for SMBs" to "AI-native creation platform for the internet economy." The acquisition of Base44 — now reaching $100M ARR in nine months under Wix ownership — is the most compelling proof point that this transition is real and monetizing faster than expected.

Website building is trending toward a commodity. Wix's response (Harmony, Base44) is strategically sound but execution-dependent.
