## 2. INDUSTRY ANALYSIS

Robinhood operates at the intersection of three converging high-growth markets:

**1. Retail Brokerage / Fintech ($600B+ TAM for the superapp)**
The U.S. retail brokerage market has been democratized since 2019's zero-commission transition. Competition has shifted to product breadth, UX, and ancillary financial services. Robinhood's mobile-first, Gen Z/Millennial-focused platform holds structural advantages in user acquisition cost among younger demographics. Industry trend toward "active-passive" investing — holding core ETF positions while actively trading crypto and event contracts — structurally aligns with Robinhood's diversified product suite.

**2. Prediction Markets / Event Contracts (400%+ volume growth 2024-2025)**
With over 12 billion contracts traded in 2025 and a vertically integrated infrastructure (CFTC-licensed MIAXdx exchange via Susquehanna JV), Robinhood has established a defensible, regulated position in the fastest-growing segment of retail finance. March Madness 2026 and the broader cultural normalization of prediction markets represent near-term seasonal tailwinds.

**3. Crypto (retail + institutional)**
Bitstamp acquisition gives Robinhood dual exposure — retail via its app and institutional B2B via Bitstamp. February 2026 total crypto volumes: $25B. However, retail crypto enthusiasm has meaningfully cooled from its 2024-2025 peak, representing a near-term headwind.

**Competitive Landscape:**
- **vs. IBKR:** Most relevant peer. IBKR trades at ~22-25x forward P/E vs. HOOD at 26.7x — small premium justified by higher growth.
- **vs. SCHW:** Schwab's 6T AUM and zero-cost model is the most formidable long-term competitive threat. HOOD's premium (P/S 14.77x vs. SCHW ~5x) reflects the growth premium.
- **vs. COIN:** HOOD is a lower-beta crypto play with brokerage stability; COIN trades at 40x+ forward P/E with pure-play crypto risk.
- **vs. Webull / eToro / Chime:** Emerging competition for the same young-demographic, mobile-first wallet.

**Key Industry Risk — PFOF Regulatory Threat:** The EU banned PFOF under MiFID III. The SEC under multiple administrations has flagged PFOF as a structural conflict of interest. A U.S. PFOF ban is not imminent but is the single largest existential business model risk — one that could reduce equity/options transaction revenue by 60-80%.
